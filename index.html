AIQ International Sales Analysis

Tools Used: Excel, SPSS, Word

The AIQ Limited project aimed to identify the most profitable international markets and key customer segments. The analysis revealed:

  • Germany, France, and Eire are the top non-UK markets for expansion.
  • Gift Vouchers are the key product driving international profitability.
  • Cluster 2 represents the most valuable non-UK customer segment.

These insights allow AIQ to prioritise marketing efforts and allocate resources effectively for strategic international growth.

Artificial Neural Network (ANN) Analysis

ANN models were used to predict sales profitability and identify the most influential factors. This revealed that product type and pricing were the strongest drivers of international performance.

Cross-Tabulation Analysis

Cross-tabulation compared sales results across countries and products, highlighting the most profitable international markets and products for expansion.

Cluster Analysis

Cluster analysis segmented customers into groups based on their buying behaviour. This helped identify the most valuable customer segments outside the UK for targeted marketing.

Personalisation & Emotional Intelligence in Luxury Chatbots

Tools Used: SPSS, Word

This project formed the basis of my MSc Dissertation, where I investigated how personalisation and emotional intelligence influence customer satisfaction with chatbots in the luxury goods market. Grounded in Social Exchange Theory, the research explored how tailored interactions and emotionally intelligent responses foster trust, satisfaction, and stronger brand-customer relationships.

A quantitative, cross-sectional survey was conducted with luxury consumers, and data was analysed using SPSS. Findings revealed that:

  • Personalisation (e.g., tailored recommendations) significantly improves customers’ sense of being valued.
  • Emotional intelligence (e.g., empathy, recognition of customer emotions) enhances overall chatbot satisfaction.
  • The social exchange mechanism mediates these relationships, showing that customers respond positively when they perceive interactions as mutually beneficial.

These insights highlight how luxury brands can optimise chatbots to deliver human-like, premium service, aligning digital experiences with the exclusivity of luxury retail.

Sentiment Analysis on Movie Reviews

Tools Used: Python, Google Colab, Pandas, Scikit-learn (CountVectorizer, MultinomialNB, train_test_split, accuracy_score)

  • View Notebook

Developed a sentiment analysis model to classify movie reviews as positive or negative. Key steps included dataset preparation, text vectorisation, model training, and evaluation. The project provided practical experience in natural language processing and demonstrated the full workflow of text classification.

Key Steps & Visuals

Key Learnings

  • Small datasets limit model accuracy and generalisation.
  • Text preprocessing is critical for machine learning models.
  • CountVectorizer cannot handle unseen words, causing misclassifications.
  • Naive Bayes is effective for simple text classification tasks.
  • Testing with new reviews highlights model limitations and areas for improvement.
  • Iterative improvements and dataset expansion are essential for robust models.

Conclusion

This project reinforced the complete workflow of text classification using Python and NLP techniques. While the model performed well on the training set, limitations with new data highlighted the importance of larger, more diverse datasets and iterative refinement. It strengthened the understanding of sentiment analysis, feature extraction, and practical machine learning applications.

Retail Sales Data Cleaning, Trend Analysis and Visualisation

Tools Used: Excel, SQL (MySQL), Tableau/Power BI

The objective of this project was to clean and analyse messy retail sales data, identify trends, and generate actionable insights. Tasks included handling missing values, removing duplicates, standardising formats, and preparing the dataset for SQL querying and visualisation. The project strengthened skills in data cleaning, analysis, and turning raw data into business-relevant insights.

Key Steps & Visuals

Key Learnings

  • Data cleaning is crucial for accurate analysis; handling duplicates, missing values, and inconsistent formats improves reliability.
  • Combining Excel and SQL allows efficient preparation and querying of datasets for insights.
  • Visualisations (heatmaps, scatter plots, histograms) help uncover trends, seasonal patterns, and the effectiveness of discounts.
  • Analysing sales trends informs business decisions like product focus, discount strategies, and inventory planning.
  • Practical experience reinforced the importance of turning data into actionable business insights.

Recommendations

  • Focus marketing and inventory efforts on high-revenue categories such as Electronics and Furniture.
  • Use moderate discounts (15–20%) for Furniture and minimal discounts for Clothing to maximise sales.
  • Plan stock and campaigns in line with seasonal peaks (e.g., December and February).
  • Continue monitoring sales trends to adjust strategies based on emerging patterns.

Conclusion

This project highlighted the importance of meticulous data cleaning, SQL-based trend analysis, and visualisation for business decision-making. The insights gained into category performance, discount effectiveness, and seasonal trends can guide strategic planning. Personally, it strengthened my skills in data preparation, analysis, and translating data into actionable business recommendations.
